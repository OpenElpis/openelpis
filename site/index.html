What's known about endocrine resistance in HR-positive breast cancer?

The vision

Knowledge breast-cancer researchers can trust — answered by AI that shows its sources.

Breast cancer is the most-researched cancer in the world — and still, thousands of papers a week, de-identified findings, and hard-won clinical insight never reach the people who could use them. OpenElpis starts here: the trustworthy middle layer for breast-cancer research — validated in, citation-grounded out.

Validated knowledge

Every contribution is identity-verified, reviewed by breast-cancer specialists, and permanently provenance-tracked. Trust is the product.

Citation-grounded AI

The copilot retrieves from a curated, peer-reviewed breast-cancer corpus and shows every source. It surfaces evidence — it never invents it.

Open & non-profit

Open-source code, open methods, transparent data integrity. Free for the research community — no paywalls, no lock-in.

Safe by design

No patient-identifiable data early; ethics and governance come first. OpenElpis offers research hypotheses — never diagnosis or treatment advice.

The road ahead

Deep in breast cancer first — then every cancer.

We prove the trust pipeline on safe, public breast-cancer knowledge, then extend the exact same machinery to other cancers.

1
Now · Foundation

Verified contributors & a breast-cancer literature copilot

Identity verification, expert review, and a citation-grounded RAG copilot over reviewed breast-cancer literature. No patient data.

2
Next · Structured data

De-identified datasets, versioned & validated

Schema-checked, de-identified breast-cancer datasets (genomics, lab values) with reproducible versioning and contributor reputation.

3
Later · Imaging & models

Breast-specific specialist models, under ethics oversight

Mammography, histopathology & omics with de-identification pipelines, data-use agreements, and IRB/ethics governance first.

4
Beyond · Expand

From breast cancer to every cancer

Once the trust pipeline is proven, the same platform extends to other cancers — and toward federated learning, where models reach hospital data while raw data never leaves the institution.
